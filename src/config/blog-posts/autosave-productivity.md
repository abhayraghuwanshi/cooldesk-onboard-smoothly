# Stop Worrying About Saving: The Magic of Browser Autosave

We all have that one friend with 500 tabs open. "I might need these!" they say.

This is **tab hoarding**, and it's driven by fear. The fear of losing context.

*   "If I close this tab, I'll never find it again."
*   "If I restart my computer, I'll lose my place."
*   "If the browser crashes, my work is gone."

This fear forces us to work in cluttered browsers, slowing down our computers and our brains.

## The Autosave Revolution

Modern productivity tools like Google Docs and Notion have trained us to expect autosave. You type, it saves. Closing the tab doesn't mean deleting the work.

But browsers are still stuck in the 90s. When you close a tab, it's gone. Unless you bookmark it (which is a graveyard for links), it becomes just another line in your history.

## Browser-Level Autosave

Imagine if your browser worked like Google Docs. Every tab you open, every window arrangement, every note—it's all saved. Automatically.

This changes how you work:

1.  **Close without Fear**: Finish a task? Close the window. Need it back tomorrow? Click "Restore Session" or search for it.
2.  **Focus on One Thing**: Clear away everything except your current project. The rest is safe.
3.  **Crash-Proof Workflow**: Power outage? Browser update? No problem. You pick up exactly where you left off.
